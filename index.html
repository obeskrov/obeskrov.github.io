about

My name is Obeskrov (the name I use on). I'm 15 years old and I live in Brazil.

I'm a student, front-end developer, and UI/UX designer. I build interfaces and often get lost in details nobody asked to exist. I start projects on impulse, get obsessed for a while, and then drop them when another idea shows up and feels more interesting.

I care more about feeling than whether something "works properly." I keep thinking about how color, movement, text, and even empty space completely change the way someone experiences an interface. To me, an interface isn't just a screen — it's almost a reflection of the person who made it. Design has never felt like decoration; it's always felt like a messy mix of psychology, identity, and sensation.

I also absolutely hate math.

I like coffee, music, anime, movies, series, reading random stuff, and writing late at night when my mind just won't shut off. I'm drawn to things with atmosphere — slightly melancholic, cold in mood, hard to explain but easy to feel.

I like UNIX-like systems, like Linux. And that's kind of contradictory: I hate having to control everything… but I also love it. Like when I break my system at 3 a.m. because of a questionable decision and still only go to sleep afterward, like nothing happened.

Sometimes I get stuck in an idea for hours because it just feels right in my head. Other times I just tear everything down and change direction without thinking too much.

I'm also into visual arts, photography, theater, stage direction, symbolism, and things that manage to transmit feeling without needing explanation. I almost never study in a traditional way. I only go deep into things when I feel like it, until I get bored, and then I drop half of it and move on like it's normal.

some things I'm interested in
  • Psychology & philosophy — emotion, identity, and how people create meaning through contradiction and uncertainty.
  • Personal work — writing, drawing, reading, and deep study.
  • Human development [0–14] — how behavior forms during childhood and shapes perception, emotion, and identity.
  • Digital aesthetics & interfaces — minimal, intentional, and expressive systems.
  • Learning deeply — understanding ideas until their structures become visible.
